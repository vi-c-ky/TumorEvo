[1]
MUTATION THEORY

Goldie J.H. & Coldman A.J. (1979). A mathematic model for relating the drug sensitivity of tumors to their spontaneous mutation rate. Cancer Treatment Reports, 63(11–12), 1727–1733. PMID: 534763

Probability of resistance increases exponentially with tumour size and division number — the justification for early, aggressive combination therapy. Model link: The μ_A·r·S and μ_B·r·S mutation terms are a direct deterministic implementation of the Goldie-Coldman stochastic model.

[2]
DOSE INTENSITY

Norton L. & Simon R. (1986). The Norton-Simon hypothesis revisited. Cancer Treatment Reports, 70(1), 163–169. PMID: 3753108

Tumour regression rate is proportional to growth rate — dose intensity must match tumour aggressiveness. Model link: Varying the proliferation rate slider against different schedules directly demonstrates this: high r requires higher dose intensity to achieve net negative growth.

[3]
CML DYNAMICS

Michor F., et al. (2005). Dynamics of chronic myeloid leukemia. Nature, 435(7046), 1267–1270. DOI: 10.1038/nature03513

Cancer stem cell hierarchy alters resistance evolution — imatinib induces remission but may not eradicate CML stem cells. Model link: The 0.95/0.90 fitness cost parameters on R_A, R_B, and R_AB approximate the reduced proliferative capacity of resistant clones from this hierarchical framework.

[4]
ADAPTIVE THERAPY

Gatenby R.A. & Brown J.S. (2020). The evolution and ecology of resistance in cancer therapy. Cold Spring Harbor Perspectives in Medicine, 10(11), a040972. DOI: 10.1101/cshperspect.a040972

Maximum tolerated doses select strongly for resistance; maintaining sensitive cells competitively suppresses resistant subclones. Model link: The shared logistic carrying capacity term (1 − N/K) encodes exactly this competitive suppression dynamic.

[5]
DOSE OPTIMISATION

Okereke I., et al. (2025). Dose intensity optimization in combination chemotherapy. Journal of Theoretical Biology, 598, 112042. DOI: 10.1016/j.jtbi.2025.112042

Therapeutic index is maximised by metronomic scheduling at intermediate mutation rates. Model link: The TI comparison chart in panel 4 directly implements the TI = (1/mean_tumour) / (toxicity+1) metric from this paper.
